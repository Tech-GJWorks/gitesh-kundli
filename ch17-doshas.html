Doshas — Honest Reading of the Shadowsदोष — सावल्यांचे प्रामाणिक वाचन

Chapter 16 walked the chart's yogas; Chapter 17 walks its doshas. A dosha (दोष) is a named astrological affliction: a specific planetary or transit-pattern that classical Jyotish has identified as producing difficulty. As with yogas, doshas come from textual citation — without a classical name and a recorded effect, an unfavorable combination is just an observation, not a dosha. Indian popular astrology has hyped several doshas (especially Manglik and Kaal-Sarp) into terrifying levels while frequently underplaying their cancellations, producing fee-driven fear that the texts themselves do not support. This chapter treats the dosha-side honestly using the same four-step scaffold as Chapter 16: (1) the exact condition the dosha describes, (2) all classical possibilities and cancellation-rules that apply (Layer 1, generic teaching), (3) the actual firing status in this chart with the evidence-chain (Layer 2, this-chart filter), and (4) a one-sentence Conclusion that compresses the section's verdict. The reader can scan only the Conclusion-lines and still receive the chart's complete dosha-verdict; the full sections reward the reader who wants to understand why the verdict is what it is. The final tally section (§17.10) summarizes which doshas truly afflict this chart and which are correctly absent — and which need calibrated remedies versus none at all.

अध्याय १६ ने कुंडलीतले योग चालले; अध्याय १७ दोष चालतो. दोष = नामांकित ज्योतिषीय पीडा — विशिष्ट ग्रह-संयोग किंवा गोचर-नमुना जो शास्त्रीय ज्योतिषाने अडचण-निर्माता म्हणून नोंदलेला. योगांप्रमाणे दोषही ग्रंथ-संदर्भातून — नाव व नोंद-फळाशिवाय अशुभ संयोग = फक्त निरीक्षण, "दोष" नव्हे. भारतीय लोकप्रिय ज्योतिषाने अनेक दोष (विशेषतः मांगलिक, कालसर्प) भयानक पातळीवर फुगवले व त्यांच्या रद्द-कारणांना दुर्लक्षित ठेवले — शुल्क-प्रेरित भीती निर्माण केली जी मूळ ग्रंथ-समर्थन देत नाहीत. हा अध्याय दोष-बाजू अध्याय १६ च्या त्याच चार-पायरी पद्धतीने प्रामाणिकपणे हाताळतो: (१) दोषाची नेमकी अटी-स्थिती, (२) सर्व शास्त्रीय शक्यता व रद्द-नियम (स्तर १), (३) कुंडलीत प्रत्यक्ष फायरिंग व पुरावा-साखळी (स्तर २), (४) एक-वाक्य निष्कर्ष. वाचक फक्त निष्कर्ष-ओळी वाचून दोष-निवाडा घेऊ शकतो; पूर्ण विभाग का ते सांगतात. अंतिम तक्ता (§१७.१०) — कोणते दोष खरोखर अडचण-निर्माते, कोणते योग्यपणे अनुपस्थित, कोणत्याला मोजका उपाय, कोणत्याला काहीच नको.

17.1 What a Dosha Is — Definition · Categories · Activation Rules१७.१ दोष म्हणजे काय — व्याख्या · प्रकार · सक्रियता-नियम

Condition / state line. A dosha is a specific, named, citable astrological affliction — a planetary configuration or transit pattern that classical texts identify as producing difficulty, when no cancellation-rule applies. Three structural elements: (a) one or more participating planets or transit factors, (b) the relevant relationship — yuti, aspect, sign-occupation, transit-overlap — and (c) a classical citation with a defined effect. Without textual citation a combination is just an unfavorable observation; with citation it becomes a named-dosha that the reader can look up, test for cancellations, and respond to with calibrated remedies.

Possibilities under this condition. Classical Jyotish recognizes dozens of doshas across five rough categories: (1) Yuti / aspect-doshas — two-planet combinations producing trouble (Shrapit = Sa+Ra or Sa+Ke; Pisaca = Me+Ra; Vish = Mo+Sa; Guru-Chandala = Ju+Ra; Grahan = Sun-or-Moon yuti node); (2) House-placement doshas — a planet sitting in a specific house from Lagna/Moon/Venus (Manglik = Mars in 1/2/4/7/8/12); (3) Whole-chart doshas — overall pattern (Kaal-Sarp = all planets on one side of Ra-Ke axis; Daridra = 11L afflicted/dushthana); (4) Nakshatra-doshas — birth-Moon in specific nakshatra (Ganda-mool = Ashvini/Ashlesha/Magha/Jyeshtha/Mula/Revati first quarters in particular); (5) Transit-doshas — gochar-pattern producing temporary affliction (Sade-Sati = Saturn in 12/1/2 from natal Moon; Dhaiya = Saturn in 4/8 from natal Moon; Ashtama-Shani = Saturn transiting 8H from Lagna). Cancellation-rules exist for nearly every dosha. The Manglik-bhanga rules are the most numerous (six classical bhangas). Kaal-Sarp is broken by any planet sitting outside the Ra-Ke arc. Pitra-dosha is mitigated by strong 9L. Shrapit is softened by Saturn or Ketu being in own/exaltation. Sade-Sati is a phase, not a permanent state, and dissolves automatically when Saturn moves on. Activation rules: yuti-doshas operate continuously throughout life but intensify during the dasha of the participating planets; house-placement doshas activate during the relevant house-significations' periods (Manglik intensifies around marriage-age and during Mars MD/AD); transit-doshas activate exactly during the transit window. Strength rules: a dosha's actual delivered impact depends on (i) shadbala of the participating planets, (ii) whether the dosha-planets are in own/friendly vs enemy/debilitated signs, (iii) benefic-aspect mitigation, (iv) divisional-chart confirmation, (v) presence of compensating yogas in the affected area. Many "feared" doshas dissolve under careful analysis; many "ignored" subtle afflictions are more real than the famous ones.

Strong possibility for this chart. This chart's dosha-list, by category: Yuti / aspect doshas — Shrapit (Sa+Ke yuti 9H, mild form due to Saturn own-nakshatra), Pisaca (Me+Ra yuti 3H, partial due to upachaya house). House-placement doshas — Manglik (Mars in 2H from Lagna, 7H from Moon, conjoined to Venus — formally triple but multiply cancelled). Whole-chart doshas — Kaal-Sarp NOT formed (planets on both sides of Ra-Ke axis, §16.10), Daridra NOT formed (11L Venus in 2H own-house-of-Sun strongly placed). Nakshatra-doshas — Ganda-mool NOT applicable (Moon's Dhanishta P3 is not a ganda-mool nakshatra). Transit-doshas — Sade-Sati Phase 3 ACTIVE (Saturn currently transiting Pisces = 2nd from natal Moon Aquarius), see §17.4 and ch25 for full transit analysis. Other — Pitra-dosha mild (Sun debilitated 4H, 9H carries Sa+Ke), Vish-yoga sign-level only (Moon in Saturn's sign Aquarius but no direct yuti), Guru-Chandala NOT formed (Jupiter 6H, Rahu 3H, no yuti or aspect), Grahan-dosha NOT formed (Sun and Moon both free from node-yuti), Kemadruma multiply-cancelled (§16.7). Net dosha-load: two mild yuti-doshas (Shrapit, Pisaca), one technical-but-cancelled house-placement-dosha (Manglik), one currently-active transit-phase (Sade-Sati Phase 3), one mild whole-chart marker (Pitra-light). None severe, none requiring expensive intervention. Most remedies needed are simple traditional practices.

Conclusion: A dosha is a named, citable affliction with classical cancellation-rules; this chart carries five mild-to-moderate doshas (Shrapit-mild, Pisaca-partial, Manglik-cancelled, Sade-Sati Phase-3 active, Pitra-light) and is correctly free of the four commonly-feared severe ones (Kaal-Sarp, Daridra, Guru-Chandala, Grahan).

अटी-स्थिती. दोष = विशिष्ट, नामांकित, शास्त्रोक्त ज्योतिषीय पीडा — ग्रह-संयोग किंवा गोचर-नमुना ज्याला शास्त्र अडचण-निर्माता मानते, जर रद्द-नियम लागू नसेल. तीन घटक: (अ) सहभागी ग्रह/गोचर-कारक, (ब) संबंध — युती, दृष्टी, राशी-स्थान, गोचर-आच्छादन, (क) निश्चित-फळासह शास्त्रीय संदर्भ. संदर्भाशिवाय संयोग = अशुभ निरीक्षण, "दोष" नव्हे; संदर्भासह नामांकित-दोष — वाचक तपासू शकतो, रद्द शोधू शकतो, मोजका उपाय करू शकतो.

शक्यता. शास्त्रीय जोतिषात दोषांचे पाच ढोबळ प्रकार: (१) युती/दृष्टी-दोष — दोन-ग्रह त्रास-संयोग (शापित = शनी+राहू/केतू; पिशाच = बुध+राहू; विष = चंद्र+शनी; गुरू-चांडाल = गुरू+राहू; ग्रहण = सूर्य-चंद्र युती राहू/केतू); (२) भाव-स्थान-दोष — विशिष्ट भावात ग्रह (मांगलिक = मंगळ १/२/४/७/८/१२ मध्ये); (३) पूर्ण-कुंडली-दोष (कालसर्प = ग्रह राहू-केतू एका बाजूला; दरिद्र = ११L बाधित/दुष्ठान); (४) नक्षत्र-दोष (गंडमूल = अश्विनी/अश्लेषा/मघा/ज्येष्ठा/मूळ/रेवती विशेष पाद); (५) गोचर-दोष (साडेसाती = शनी १२/१/२ चंद्रापासून; धैय्या = शनी ४/८ चंद्रापासून; अष्टम-शनी = शनी ८ लग्नापासून). रद्द-नियम जवळपास प्रत्येक दोषासाठी: मांगलिक-बंगा सहा क्लासिकल; कालसर्प कोणताही ग्रह कमानेबाहेर असल्यास तुटतो; पितृ बलवान ९L ने शमते; शापित शनी/केतू स्व/उच्च असल्यास मऊ; साडेसाती कायमची नव्हे, शनी पुढे जाताच विरून जाते. सक्रियता: युती-दोष आयुष्यभर पण सहभागी ग्रहांच्या दशेत तीव्र; भाव-स्थान-दोष संबंधित भावाच्या वय-काळात (मांगलिक विवाह-वयाच्या आसपास व मंगळ MD/AD मध्ये); गोचर-दोष फक्त गोचर-खिडकीत. बल-नियम: प्रत्यक्ष प्रभाव — (i) षड्बल, (ii) सहभागी ग्रह स्व/मित्र की शत्रू/नीच, (iii) शुभ-दृष्टी-शमन, (iv) अंश-कुंडली, (v) सामना देणार्‍या योगांची उपस्थिती. अनेक "भीतीचे" दोष काळजीपूर्वक विश्लेषणाखाली विरून जातात; अनेक "दुर्लक्षित" सूक्ष्म पीडा प्रसिद्ध दोषांपेक्षा खर्‍या ठरतात.

या कुंडलीत प्रत्यक्ष. कुंडली-दोष-यादी, प्रकारानुसार: युती/दृष्टी — शापित (शनी+केतू ९ युती, शनीच्या स्व-नक्षत्राने सौम्य), पिशाच (बुध+राहू ३ युती, उपचय-भावाने आंशिक). भाव-स्थान — मांगलिक (मंगळ २ लग्नापासून, ७ चंद्रापासून, शुक्र-युती — औपचारिक त्रिकोणी पण अनेक-वेळा रद्द). पूर्ण-कुंडली — कालसर्प अनुपस्थित (§१६.१०), दरिद्र अनुपस्थित (११L शुक्र २ सिंहात बलवान). नक्षत्र — गंडमूल लागू नाही (धनिष्ठा पाद ३ गंडमूल नाही). गोचर — साडेसाती टप्पा ३ सक्रिय (शनी मीनेत = कुंभापासून २रा), §१७.४ व अध्याय २५. इतर — पितृ-दोष सौम्य (४व्या नीच सूर्य, ९ शनी+केतू), विष-योग राशी-स्तरीय (चंद्र शनीच्या कुंभेत पण थेट युती नाही), गुरू-चांडाल अनुपस्थित, ग्रहण-दोष अनुपस्थित, केमद्रुम अनेक-वेळा रद्द (§१६.७). निव्वळ दोष-भार: दोन सौम्य युती (शापित, पिशाच), एक तांत्रिक-पण-रद्द भाव-स्थान (मांगलिक), एक सध्या सक्रिय गोचर-टप्पा (साडेसाती ३), एक सौम्य पूर्ण-कुंडली (पितृ-हलका). कोणीही गंभीर नाही, कोणालाही महाग हस्तक्षेपाची गरज नाही. आवश्यक उपाय बहुतेक साधे पारंपरिक.

निष्कर्ष: दोष = नामांकित-शास्त्रोक्त-पीडा रद्द-नियमांसह; ही कुंडली पाच सौम्य-ते-मध्यम दोष (शापित-सौम्य, पिशाच-आंशिक, मांगलिक-रद्द, साडेसाती-३-सक्रिय, पितृ-हलका) वाहते व चार सामान्य-भीतीचे गंभीर दोष (कालसर्प, दरिद्र, गुरू-चांडाल, ग्रहण) योग्यपणे अनुपस्थित.

17.2 Manglik / Mangal-Dosha — Mars in 2H from Lagna · 7H from Moon · 1H from Venus१७.२ मांगलिक / कुज-दोष — मंगळ २H लग्न, ७H चंद्र, १H शुक्र

Condition / state line. Mars in the 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, or 12th house from the Lagna (strict-classical) OR from the Moon (Phaladeepika-extension) OR — in the strictest schools — from Venus (Brihat-Parashara variant for marriage-specific analysis). The severity-ordering of the six houses for marriage is: 7th (most severe — direct hit on spouse), 8th, 4th, 12th, 2nd, 1st (least severe). Mars in any one of these positions makes the chart "Manglik" or "Mangal"; the popular north-Indian term "Manglik" and the south-Indian "Kuja-dosha" name the same condition.

Possibilities under this condition. The dosha's classical effect is restricted to marriage-affairs: spousal conflict, marital delay, separation, widowhood-risk in extreme cases, partner's health-difficulty. Mars represents fire, aggression, drive, and assertion; in marriage-related houses his fire is read as friction. Six Manglik-bhanga (cancellation) rules: Bhanga 1 — Mars in own sign (Aries, Scorpio) or exaltation sign (Capricorn). Mars's fire is "at home" and behaves constructively. Bhanga 2 — Mars conjunct or aspected by Jupiter or Venus. Benefic-companionship redirects Mars's fire. Bhanga 3 — Mars aspected by the 7th lord, or 7th house-occupant, or spouse-significator (Venus for male, Jupiter for female). The marriage-house's own factor controls Mars. Bhanga 4 — Both spouses are Manglik (cross-cancellation). This is the most popular bhanga in marriage-matchmaking. Bhanga 5 — Mars in 2H specifically is held by modern schools (B.V. Raman, K.N. Rao, Sanjay Rath) as a soft-form because the 2H is about family-and-speech rather than directly about spouse; the friction emerges in family-conversations rather than spouse-relationship. Bhanga 6 — Mars as functional benefic / yogakaraka for the Lagna. When Mars is the chart's yogakaraka, his natural-malefic-quality is overridden by his functional-benefic-role, dramatically reducing dosha-effect. Activation: Manglik intensifies (a) around marriage-age (typically late 20s), (b) during Mars MD/AD, (c) during gochar Mars transiting the 1/2/4/7/8/12 houses or Mars-related natal positions, (d) when both partners check their charts and discover Manglik status. Cancellation-of-cancellation: if multiple bhangas are present and the participating planet (Jupiter/Venus/lord-of-7) is itself debilitated or combust, some schools dilute the bhanga's strength.

Strong possibility for this chart. Mars at 1° Leo. From Lagna (Cancer), Leo is 2H = Manglik from Lagna. From Moon (Aquarius), Leo is 7H = Manglik from Moon (the most-severe-position by classical-severity-ordering!). From Venus (Leo, same sign as Mars), Mars is 1H = Manglik from Venus. Triple Manglik on paper — and one of the three is from the most-severe house (7H-from-Moon). However, the bhanga-test produces multiple cancellations: Bhanga 1 — Mars in Leo, which is Sun's sign and friendly to Mars (Sun is Mars's friend). Not own/exalted, but friendly. Partial cancellation only. Bhanga 2 — Mars conjunct Venus in 2H. Full cancellation fires. Also, Mars is aspected by Jupiter from 6H via Jupiter's 9th-special-aspect (6+9-1=14 mod 12 = 2 → Jupiter's 9th-aspect lands on 2H, hitting Mars directly). Second full cancellation fires. Bhanga 3 — 7th lord = Saturn. Saturn in 9H. Saturn's aspects from 9H are 3-7-10 special, which from 9 give 11, 3, 6; not 2H. Does not fire. Bhanga 4 — partner-Manglik-status to be tested at marriage-time (cross-cancellation if applicable). Bhanga 5 — Mars in 2H specifically, which modern schools soften. Soft-form cancellation fires. Bhanga 6 — Mars is Cancer Lagna's yogakaraka (5L+10L). Strongest cancellation of all, fires fully. Net cancellation: four bhangas fire (Bhanga 1 partial, Bhanga 2 full × two-fold via Venus-conjunction and Jupiter-aspect, Bhanga 5 soft-form, Bhanga 6 yogakaraka). The remaining Manglik-effect after this dense cancellation-stack is mild and behavioral, not catastrophic: slight delay of marriage (28–32 age-range, also supported by Saturn-in-7th-lordship), passionate-intensity in relationship (Mars-signature), need for both partners to have independent outlets for high-energy drives, periodic Mars-transit months bringing temporary friction that passes with patience. The marriage itself is not under structural-risk — Mars-yogakaraka in 2H actually blesses family-wealth and spouse-stability rather than harming it. See ch28 for full marriage-analysis cross-reference and ch11 §11.17 for Mars+Venus 2H mechanics. The combination of Mars MD + Mars transit over 2H is a Manglik-intensification window in 2026-Apr through 2026-Jun (Mars gochar in Leo Apr-Jun 2026) — useful for noting that any marriage-related decision in this short window benefits from extra patience.

17.6 Shrapit-Dosha — Saturn yuti Ketu in 9H Pisces१७.६ शापित-दोष — शनी-केतू ९H मीन युती

Condition / state line. Shrapit-Dosha (शापित-दोष — "cursed-yoga") forms when Saturn is in close yuti with either Rahu or Ketu (Sa+Ra or Sa+Ke), or under strong mutual aspect. The combination is said to bring "karmic curse" residue — a sense of doing what is right yet meeting persistent obstruction, slow-but-heavy progress in the affected house's life-area, recurring tests that feel unjustly difficult, occasional health-and-sleep disturbances.

Possibilities under this condition. The dosha can manifest in different intensities based on (a) whether Saturn yuti Rahu vs Saturn yuti Ketu (Rahu-form: external entanglements, foreign-or-unconventional-source obstacles; Ketu-form: internal-detachment, burnt-out-merit, dharma-house heaviness), (b) the house of the yuti (kendra and trikona produce more far-reaching effects than dushthana, but dushthana-Shrapit can be the most karmically-intense per Brihat-Parashara), (c) the sign — own/exaltation/friendly softens, enemy/debilitated intensifies, (d) the nakshatra-pada of both planets — same-nakshatra yuti is the most-intense form, different-nakshatra is softer. Effects: heaviness in the area of the yuti, recurring tests, slow-but-stable progress, late maturation, asceticism-tinge, occasional unexplained obstruction at the moment of breakthrough, dharma-or-spiritual area as the lifelong-focus when the yuti is in 9H, identity-or-foundation issues when in 1H, family-or-speech difficulties when in 2H, etc. Mitigations: Saturn in own/exaltation/own-nakshatra softens significantly; Ketu in friendly sign (Jupiter-ruled Sagittarius/Pisces) softens; benefic-aspect on the yuti from Jupiter or Venus mitigates; strong 9L (for 9H Shrapit) compensates; Saturn-Ketu in own-pada of either planet's nakshatra reduces dosha-intensity. Calibrated remedies: Hanuman-worship (the deity that softens both Saturn and Ketu), Saturday-shanti practices, mantra-japa of Maha-Mrityunjaya for general affliction-softening, Sani-Tail-abhyanga (sesame-oil application on Saturdays), donation of black-and-iron items on Saturdays, simplicity-of-living practices (Saturn-Ketu yuti rewards minimalism). Activation: intensifies during Saturn MD/AD, Ketu MD/AD, gochar Saturn or Ketu transits over the natal yuti, Sade-Sati windows. Comparison to Sannyasa-yoga: the same Sa+Ke yuti in a dharma-house produces Shrapit-dosha from one reading and Sannyasa-yoga from another; modern moderate-school astrologers note that the same combination can produce both effects simultaneously — the heaviness IS the spiritual-pull, the obstruction IS the dharma-deepening.

Strong possibility for this chart. Shrapit-Dosha fires in MILD form via Sa+Ke yuti in 9H Pisces. Saturn at ~degree-in-Pisces with own-nakshatra Uttara Bhadrapada; Ketu at 14° Pisces (likely in Uttara Bhadrapada or Purva Bhadrapada depending on exact degree). Intensifiers present: (i) yuti in dushthana-from-Lagna-by-some-counts (9H is trikona but also "from-the-other-side" the long-distance-direction-house — depending on framework); (ii) Ketu's nodal-quality of dharma-burnout meeting Saturn's discipline-burden. Mitigators present: (i) Saturn in own nakshatra Uttara Bhadrapada — major mitigator; the Shrapit's "curse" is softened because the disciplinarian is operating from his own well-known territory rather than from foreign ground; (ii) Ketu in friendly Pisces (Jupiter-ruled) — softens the burnt-out edge; (iii) 9L Jupiter in own sign Sagittarius — the most-important mitigator: the house's lord is at maximum strength so the dosha's heaviness gets channelled into the chart's Sannyasa-yoga + Mahanidhi-yogi signature rather than into raw affliction; (iv) simultaneous Sannyasa-yoga reading — what one school calls Shrapit-dosha, another school reads as the chart's premier dharma-signature (§16.8 cross-reference).
